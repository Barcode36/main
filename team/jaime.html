PROJECT: ExpenseTracker

Overview

This portfolio aims to document the contributions made by Jaime Chow Wen Juan to the ExpenseTracker.

The ExpenseTracker is a desktop expense tracking application developed to aid users in tracking their expenses and save money. The user interacts with it using a Command Line Interface (CLI), and it has a Graphical User Interface (GUI) created with JavaFX library. It is written in Java, and has about 20 kLoC.

Challenges

  • Dynamic Text Size Change - As this is not a standard feature of any text-based JavaFX class, it had to manually implemented. In addition, TextFlow objects do not truncate any text overflows, but instead alters the TextView width. Attempting to dynamically increase or decrease the size of the text based on the TextFlow#width proved difficult and inconsistent when coupled with the animation.

  • Testing - There were issues that arose during testing due to the addition of animations. With how animations were implemented, the tests methods would check the values of the progressBar and Text objects before they were correctly updated. Ultimately, a decision to call `Thread#sleep# for the duration of the animation was made.

    • Notification - Stores the message to be sent to the user. There are two types of Notification: TipNotification and WarningNotification.

    • NotificationHandler - Handles the storage and creation of Notification objects.

    • NotificationPanel and NotificationCard - GUI Elements that displays the Notifications to the user.

    • XmlAdaptedNotificationHandler, JsonTipsStorage and XmlAdaptedNotificationHandler - Manages the saving and reading on Notifications.

The instance field was initially initialized to LocalDate#now(), resulting in many errors due to the dynamic nature of the instance field. However, a Model created via getTypicalModel would not call Model#loadData. There were instances where these differently instantiated Model objects would be compared.

  • Notification Storage - The given loadDataFromStorage had difficulty reading a list of Notifications into the XmlAdaptedNotificationHandler. Thus, the list had to be separated and manually added to a newly instantiated ExpenseTracker object.

Setting a total budget: setBudget

Sets totalBudget for Expense Tracker.

The Expense Tracker will warn you when your total expenditure exceeds the total budget that is set.

Format: setBudget MONEY_WITH_TWO_DECIMAL_PLACES

Examples:

  • setBudget 2.00
    Sets a totalBudget of $2.00.

  • setBudget 20.00
    Sets a totalBudget of $20.00.

  • The setBudget command is case sensitive.

  • MONEY_WITH_TWO_DECIMAL_PLACES must be a positive number with two decimal places.

Setting a recurring frequency for totalBudget: setRecurrenceFrequency

Sets the recurrence frequency for the totalBudget of Expense Tracker.

Expense Tracker will reset your spending after every FREQUENCY

Format: setRecurrenceFrequency [hrs/HOURS] [min/MINUTES] [sec/SECONDS]

Examples:

  • setRecurrenceFrequency hrs/1
    Sets the spending of totalBudget to reset every 1 hour

  • setRecurrenceFrequency hrs/1 min/30
    Sets the spending of totalBudget reset every 1 hour and 30 minutes

  • The setRecurrenceFrequency command is case sensitive.

  • HOURS/MINUTES/SECONDS must be a positive number.

Adding a Category Budget: setCategoryBudget

Adds a Budget to limit spending on a certain category.

If spending exceeds Category budget, a warning similar to 4.10 will be shown.

Sum of all category budgets cannot exceed total budget of Expense Tracker.

Format: setCategoryBudget c/CATEGORY b/BUDGET

Example:

  • setCategoryBudget c/School b/10.00
    Sets a budget of $10 to the School category.

Valid CATEOGRY and BUDGET follows the syntax listed under 4.5 and 4.10
